Week Two

Prompt: What are you planning to do for project 1? What content do you plan to show? How will you make each page unique while maintaining a cohesive design across the project? How will your color palette, imagery, and typography choice complement your subject matter?

Response: For my first project, I will be creating a small overview of modular synthesis is, for the intended audience of people just sticking their toes in. The home page will be a clean black, with only essential words and elements to keep it as simple as possible for a topic so dense and complicated. Hovering over elements will activate them with small gifs to give a better idea of how waveforms shape the sounds they produce. I'm also considering adding audio files that can be played to add another layer to this concept. Typography will remain fairly standard and grounded. Color palette will be bright neon colors to highlight important words/interactive elements. All sub-pages will remain present, visible, and accessible to the user in one frame.

Week Three

Prompt: Clearly imagine one user who'll love to use your Project 1 website. This user could be you, it could be someone similar to you, it could be someone completely opposite from you, it could even be a non-human animal or an alien or an amoeba. You get to choose. Really imagine what they are like in their mind. What do they spend their time on? what are their hobbies, clothes, music, favorite foods, books, tv. what's their personality like. Once you've imagined this person. write about them. just a paragraph, three to five sentences. try to have fun with it.

Response: I hope the person who finds my site is curious. They don't hope to find every small possibility detailed before them. Rather, they want to be gently guided into learning and experimenting for themselves. To wonder is to grow, and modular synthesis feeds this necessity for musicians who have prior knowledge of the scape.
